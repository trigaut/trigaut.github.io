Description
 

The main focus of my researches is stochastic optimization. I currently work in a R&D company named Efficacity. Our aim is to support actors, from industry and public sector, where they can make a change for cities energy efficiency. I develop methods to optimize energy systems in real time in the presence of uncertainty. I applied these methods mainly to sizing and control of local energy systems with renewables. My PhD thesis advisor in stochastic optimization was Jean Philippe Chancelier from CERMICS. My advisor for models calibration was Frédéric Bourquin from IFSTTAR.
